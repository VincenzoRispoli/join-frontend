Help

Welcome to the help page for Join, your guide to using our kanban project management tool. Here we'll provide an overvoew on what Join is, how it can benefit you, and how to use it

What is Join?

Join is a kanban-based project management tool that designed and built by a group of dedicated students as part of their Web Development Bootcamp at the Developer Akademie

Kanban, a Japanese term meaning "billboard", is a highly effective method to visualize work, limit work-in-progress and maximize efficiency (or flow). Join leverage the principles of kanban to help users manage their tasks and projects in an intuitive, visual interface.

It is important to note that Joinis designed as an educational exercise and is not intented for extensive business usage. While we strive to ensure the best possible user experience, we cannot guarantee consistent availability, reliability, accuracy or other aspects of quality regarding Join

How to use it?

Here is a step by step guide to use Join

  1. Exploring the Board

    When you log into Joinyou will find a default board.This board represents your project and contains for default lists: "To-Do", "In Progress", "Await Feedback", and "Done"

  2. Creating Contacts

    In Joinyou can add contact to collaborate with your projects. Go to the contact section, click to "Add new contact", and fill the required informations. Once added, these contacts can be assigned tasks and they can interact with the tasks on the board

  3. Adding Cards

    Now that you've added your contacts, you can start adding cards. Can represents individual tasks. Click the "+" button under the appropriate list to create a new card. Fill the task details in the card, like task title, description, due date, assignees etc.

  4. Moving Cards

    As the task moves from one stage to another, you can reflect that by dragging and dropping the card from one list to another

  5. Deleting Cards

    Once a task is completed, you can either move in to the "Done" list or delete it. Deleting a card will permanently remove from the board. Please exercise caution when deleting cards, as this action is irriversible.

    Remember that using Join effectively requires constantly updates from you and your Team, to ensure the board reflects the current state of your project.
